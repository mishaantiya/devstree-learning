About the flowers

Flowers are the reproductive organs of plants. They play a vital ecological role by producing seeds and providing food for pollinators like bees and butterflies. Beyond their environmental importance, flowers add beauty to nature and are widely used in human life for gifts, decoration, and perfumes.

Rose

Known as the "queen of flowers", the Rose is deeply symbolic of love and compassion. They come in a wide variety of colors, including red, pink, yellow, and white, and are popular in both gardens and bouquets.

Lotus

Often found floating on the surface of calm lakes and ponds, the Lotus is a revered aquatic flower. It is well known for its broad, waxy leaves and beautiful pink or white petals that symbolize purity and grace.

Sunflower

With their bright, striking yellow petals, Sunflowers are famous for following the path of the sun, a process known as heliotropism. They are grown for their beauty but also harvested for their seeds and healthy oils.
