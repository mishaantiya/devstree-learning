A flowers:

Biological Anatomy

A complete flower typically consists of four main circular levels around a central stalk. These parts include the sepals, which support and protect the unopened bud; the petals, which are usually brightly colored to attract pollinators; the stamens, which present the pollen; and the female gynoecia.

Historically, societies have assigned specific symbolic meanings to different blooms. For example, in the Victorian era, flowers were used as a form of nonverbal communication. Today, flowers remain an important economic and aesthetic resource, utilized in floriculture, perfume manufacturing, and as vital food sources for pollinators.
